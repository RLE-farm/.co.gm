🌱 Welcome to Rural Livelihood Enhancement Farm

Rural Livelihood Enhancement Farm is a dedicated agribusiness located in Kiang Kwinella (Sansang‑kono), Lower River Region, The Gambia. We operate an integrated farming system that produces vegetable fruits, poultry (chicken & eggs), baked bread, and animal fattening. Our 59,000m² farm is designed to supply fresh, high‑quality products to the local and regional markets year‑round.

With a clear commitment to sustainability and community development, we leverage climate‑smart practices and a skilled management team to ensure consistent production, food security, and economic growth for the region.

📖 About Us

Rural Livelihood Enhancement Farm is a sole proprietorship (Limited Liability Corporation) established to produce and supply vegetable fruits, fattened livestock, poultry products, and bakery items. Our integrated system combines crop farming, poultry, animal husbandry, agroforestry, and a bakery to maximise efficiency and minimise waste.

Our products include:

  • Fresh vegetables – tomato, onion, okra, garden egg, lettuce, pepper, etc.
  • Poultry – broiler chickens and eggs
  • Fruits – orange, lemon, mango, papaya
  • Bread (flour) – freshly baked
  • Agro‑products – poultry manure for fertiliser

Vision: To become the leading producer and supplier of integrated farm products in the Kiang Central markets and the Lower River Region.

Mission: To produce affordable, nutritious, and safe agricultural products using sustainable, integrated farming practices that empower local communities.

Our keys to success: Strict financial controls, a relentless pursuit of market visibility, and an unwavering commitment to 100% customer satisfaction.

⚠️ The Challenge

The Lower River Region faces significant challenges that hinder agricultural productivity and youth empowerment:

  • Neglected arable land – many productive surfaces are abandoned.
  • Youth unemployment and migration – a mindset of seeking opportunities abroad leaves many idle.
  • Low agricultural productivity – due to lack of integrated systems and modern techniques.
  • Seasonal supply gaps – irregular production leads to food shortages and price spikes.

Rural Livelihood Enhancement Farm addresses these issues by converting unused land into a productive, year‑round integrated farm, creating jobs, and supplying consistent, quality produce to local and regional markets.

💡 Our Approach

  • Integrated farming system – combining crops, poultry, livestock, beekeeping, and bakery to create synergies and reduce costs.
  • Year‑round production – using efficient irrigation and planning to ensure continuous supply.
  • Direct market engagement – supplying retailers, wholesalers, and institutions directly.
  • Quality and packaging – delivering fresh, well‑packed products that build trust and loyalty.
  • Community empowerment – employing local youth and providing training to enhance livelihoods.

This approach ensures that we not only produce food but also contribute to economic development, food security, and environmental sustainability in the region.

📊 Market Opportunity

Our target customers include:

  • Supplement companies – using our produce as raw materials.
  • District and regional traders – who buy in bulk.
  • Local retailers and vendors – serving community markets.
  • Individual consumers – seeking fresh, healthy food.

The market for integrated farm products is growing, with over 90% of Gambian households consuming dietary supplements and fresh produce. Our strategic location near Brikama, Serrekunda, and the Cassamance region gives us a competitive edge in distribution.

Marketing strategies: Participation in trade fairs (“Lumoo”), advertising in trade magazines, social media (Facebook, WhatsApp), signboards, and building strong customer relationships through consistent quality and fair pricing.

⚙️ Operations & Production Plan

Our production process follows a structured cycle to ensure efficiency and quality:

  • Land preparation – clearing, ploughing, and organic manure application.
  • Sowing and transplanting – using high‑quality seeds and seedlings.
  • Irrigation and water management – efficient watering systems.
  • Fertilisation and soil care – compost and controlled chemical inputs.
  • Pest and disease control – integrated pest management (IPM).
  • Weeding and maintenance – regular manual weeding and crop care.
  • Harvesting – at optimal maturity with careful handling.
  • Post‑harvest handling – sorting, grading, and safe storage.
  • Packaging and sales – attractive packaging and direct market delivery.

We also manage poultry (broilers and layers), animal fattening, and bakery operations as part of our integrated system, ensuring diverse income streams and resource optimisation.
